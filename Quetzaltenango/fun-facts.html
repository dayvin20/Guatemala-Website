Quetzaltenango

Xela is one of the highest cities in Central America, sitting at around 2,300 metres (7,546 feet). Xela is its much shorter nickname, derived from the old Mayan name for the city, Xelaju. It’s pronounced “sheh-la”, so make sure to say it that way if you want to sound like a local.Xela’s range of Spanish schools are a big draw for many Westerners, offering a great way to improve your Spanish skills at a fraction of the cost of tuition back home.
