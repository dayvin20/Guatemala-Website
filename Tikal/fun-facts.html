Tikal

Tikal reached its apogee during the Classic Period, c. 200 to 900 AD. During this time, the city dominated much of the Maya region politically, economically, and militarily, while interacting with areas throughout Mesoamerica. There is evidence that Tikal was conquered by Teotihuacan in the 4th century CE.[5] Following the end of the Late Classic Period, no new major monuments were built at Tikal and there is evidence that elite palaces were burned.
